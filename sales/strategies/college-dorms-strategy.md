## 1. Why College Dorms Are Perfect for Smart Vending

### The Dream Location
College dorms are arguably the **single best placement** for smart vending machines. Here's why:

- **24/7 Demand:** Students eat at ALL hours. The 11pm-3am window is a gold mine that dining halls don't serve
- **Captive Audience:** 1,000+ residents per building with no car and no nearby stores = guaranteed foot traffic
- **Young, Tech-Savvy Demo:** 18-24 year olds PREFER touchscreen, app-based, contactless payment ‚Äî they expect it
- **Repeat Customers:** Same people walking past your machine 4-8 times per day for 9 months
- **Social Proof / Virality:** One student posts about the "cool AI vending machine" and the whole dorm knows
- **Meal Plan Gaps:** Dining halls close at 8-9pm. Students still need food. That's YOUR window
- **Toiletry / Convenience Items:** Forgot shampoo? Phone charger died? Need a late-night energy drink? Vending handles it
- **Institutional Revenue Share:** Universities LOVE passive revenue streams ‚Äî you're literally bringing them free money

### Unit Economics (Per Machine, Per Dorm)
- Average dorm: 400-800 students
- Estimated daily transactions: 50-120 per machine
- Average transaction: $3.50-$5.00
- Monthly gross per machine: $5,250-$18,000
- After COGS + rev share: $2,000-$8,000 net/month
- ROI timeline: 3-6 months per machine

## 6. Navigating University Procurement

### NSHE System (Covers UNLV, CSN, Nevada State)

**Step 1: Register as NSHE Supplier**
- Go to **https://suppliers.nevada.edu**
- Create account in SReg system
- List commodity offerings (vending services, micro-markets, food service equipment)
- **One registration covers ALL 8 NSHE institutions** ‚Äî huge advantage

**Step 2: Understanding the Process**
- NSHE institutions follow Nevada state procurement law
- Contracts under $50,000 may be approved at the department level
- Larger contracts typically require RFP (Request for Proposal) process
- **Vending/food service contracts are usually handled by Auxiliary Services**

**Step 3: Multiple Entry Points**
1. **Formal RFP Route:** Watch for posted RFPs on NSHE/UNLV procurement sites
2. **Direct Pitch:** Contact auxiliary services/housing and propose a pilot
3. **Student Government Route:** Get CSUN to request smart vending ‚Üí university responds
4. **Facilities Route:** Contact facilities management about replacing aging vending equipment

### Private Schools (Touro, Roseman)
- No NSHE bureaucracy ‚Äî direct pitch to administration
- Usually just need approval from facilities/operations manager
- Can sign agreement directly with campus contact
- Faster timeline: pitch ‚Üí decision in 2-4 weeks

### The Pilot Program Hack
Universities are risk-averse. They hate committing to new vendors. **The solution: a free pilot.**
- "Let us put ONE machine in your highest-traffic dorm lobby for 90 days, completely free"
- "If students love it, we expand. If not, we remove it at zero cost to you"
- This bypasses the full RFP process (small enough to be a trial/demo)
- Gets the machine on campus ‚Üí data proves the case ‚Üí full contract follows

## 7. Pricing Strategy for Universities

### Revenue Share Model (Recommended)
| Tier | University Share | Kand√© Share | When |
|------|----------------|-------------|------|
| **Pilot (0-90 days)** | 0% | 100% | Free trial period |
| **Standard** | 10-15% of gross | 85-90% | Normal operation |
| **Premium Placement** | 15-20% of gross | 80-85% | Student Union, Library lobby |
| **Exclusive Campus Deal** | 12-18% of gross | 82-88% | Multi-machine campus-wide |

### Why Revenue Share > Flat Fee
- Universities prefer % of sales (no upfront cost, scales with usage)
- Aligns incentives ‚Äî they want the machine to succeed too
- Common industry standard ‚Äî Aramark/Canteen do the same
- Makes the "zero risk" pitch credible

### Student Discount Integration
- Consider RebelCash / student ID integration ‚Üí 5-10% student discount
- "Students pay less, university earns commission, everybody wins"
- Creates loyalty and increases transaction volume
- Positions Kand√© as student-friendly
