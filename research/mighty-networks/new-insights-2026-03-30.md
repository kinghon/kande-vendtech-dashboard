## 🔴 HIGH-IMPACT — New to Kande Strategy

### 1. Vistar Discount Tiers & Ordering Optimization
- **Off-invoice discounts are tiered**: 2% off orders over $1,500 (Modern Amenities exclusive)
- **Stack discounts**: Pre-buy deal + off-invoice discount can "double dip" on same order
- **Demand forecasting**: Tell your opco your weekly volume per SKU → they load it into "Clarity" system → you get priority fill instead of getting cut
- **Safety stock trap**: New operators start on safety stock only. Call opco and declare your volumes to get upgraded
- **Vistar Customer First portal**: Has full product catalog (exportable), cut reports, live chat
- **Action for Kande:** When we start ordering from Vistar, cluster orders above $1,500 threshold. Call opco to declare volumes immediately — don't wait for them to figure it out.

### 2. SandStar = Only True AI Platform
- **SandStar**: Only machine with REAL active AI — no human review. Camera/algorithm handles all transactions.
- **365/MicroMart/HaHa**: All use human review teams (overseas) for transaction approval. "Passive AI" is marketing.
- **HaHa**: Testing true AI (SandStar-style) later in 2026.
- **Action for Kande:** Use "True AI" as a differentiator in pitches. When property managers ask about technology, explain that most "smart" machines still use humans to review every transaction — ours don't.

### 3. Revenue Share Negotiation — 5-Location Deal
- Community operator asked about 15% rev share demand from a 5-location luxury apartment chain (265+ units each)
- **Community consensus**: Counter with tiered rev share maxing at 10%. For 5 locations, push for volume discount. "10% of zero is zero" — being local and reliable matters more than rev share percentage.
- **Action for Kande:** When negotiating multi-location deals, always counter with tiered structure. Anchor at 5%, cap at 10% for portfolio deals.

### 4. Bottler Lockout Zones (Coke/Pepsi)
- Coke and Pepsi have **zip-code contracts** that block Vistar distribution in certain areas
- If you can't order through Vistar, contact local bottler directly
- Dr Pepper: Market-specific restrictions (some markets bags only via Pepsi)
- **Action for Kande:** Verify with Vistar opco whether Las Vegas is in a lockout zone for Coke/Pepsi BEFORE assuming we can order through them. May need direct bottler relationships.

### 5. Pepsi Commercial Address Requirement

### 6. Chain Location Multiplier Effect — Proven
- Operator landed 3rd Willow Bridge apartment, 2 more in proposal stage from same chain
- Once you prove success at one property, the same management company fast-tracks additional locations
- **Action for Kande:** Every new contract should trigger immediate research into the management company's full portfolio. Pitch sister properties within 2 weeks of first install.

### 7. Machine Decision Framework (from Decision Guides)
The community's machine selection guide:
1. Decide what KIND of locations you're building around (fewer strong placements vs distributed route)
2. Evaluate the PROPERTY first — not the hardware
3. Don't oversize before the location earns it (easier to add capacity than shrink)
4. Choose ONE ecosystem and learn it across 3-5 placements before mixing
- **Action for Kande:** Commit to one machine ecosystem for first 5 locations. Don't mix HaHa/SandStar/365 early — operational complexity kills margins.

### 8. Inventory Ordering Discipline
- **Protect bestsellers first** — prevent repeat stockouts, don't keep everything equally full
- **Let slow movers reveal themselves** — single weeks are misleading, look for repeated patterns
- **Separate "feeding the machine" from "testing"** — core products keep revenue stable, test products are limited and reviewed separately
- **Fewer decisions per visit = better** — discipline is about reducing noise, not optimizing every item
- **Action for Kande:** Establish par levels by SKU after first month. Track stockouts in a simple spreadsheet. Cut bottom 3 sellers monthly, double down on top sellers.

### 9. Scaling Transition Framework
Course content covers 4 critical scaling lessons:
1. **Removing Yourself as the Bottleneck** — when effort alone stops working
2. **Systems vs Heroics** — building processes that don't depend on you
3. **Delegation without Chaos** — hiring without losing quality
4. **Designing for Predictability and Calm** — making the business boring (in a good way)
- **Action for Kande:** We're at stage 1. Kurtis is doing everything. First hire should be a stocker. Document the restocking process BEFORE hiring so the system exists independent of the person.

### 10. SandStar Skinny Can Pushers
