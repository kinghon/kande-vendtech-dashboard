## 1. PRODUCT SOURCING & DISTRIBUTOR RELATIONSHIPS

### Vistar/USG (Jeff Scherdin — March 11, 2026 VP Weekly)
- **Off-invoice discounts are tiered by order size** — stay above threshold or you lose the tier
  - Example: 2% off orders over $1,500 (MA-exclusive deal, not standard national pricing)
  - Tactic: Cluster two orders into one to hit the threshold rather than placing multiple small orders
- **Pre-buy deals**: Seasonal/limited items require advance commitment — check Mighty Networks for announcements
- **Safety stock problem**: New operators start on safety stock only; to get priority fill, call your opco and tell them your weekly volume for each SKU
- **"Double dipping"**: Stack pre-buy discount + off-invoice order discount on the same order
- **Demand forecasting**: Opco loads your demand into their "Clarity" system → they pre-purchase for you → you stop getting cut
- **Vistar Customer First portal**: Has full product list (export as Excel), cut reports, customer service chat
- **Product naming weirdness**: Use Vent Market platform to browse (cleaner naming), then reverse-engineer into Vistar catalog

### Bottler Lockouts (Soda)
- Coke and Pepsi have zip-code contracts that block Vistar distribution in certain markets
- If you can't order Coke/Pepsi through Vistar, contact your local bottler directly
- Dr. Pepper: Market-specific restrictions (Chicago: bags only via Pepsi, not cans)
- Verify with opco whether it's a lockout vs just out-of-stock before giving up

### Rebel/Modern Amenities Program
- Rebel ID numbers being issued — allows ordering off Vent Market platform
- Modern Amenities manages the ordering flow; you'll get email with Rebel ID + process
- Pricing advantage: MA bulk pricing passed through fully (no markup) — competitive vs national pricing

### Pepsi Ordering
- New Pepsi relationship coming online — commercial addresses required for approval
- Expected live by early April; await email from Modern Amenities with 24hr notice

## 2. TECHNOLOGY PLATFORMS (Vending Machines / AI)

### AI Payment Systems Comparison
- **SandStar**: Only TRUE active AI — no human review required; camera/algorithm handles all transactions
  - Lower per-transaction fees (not paying overseas approval team)
  - Most advanced; Mike (Anthony's partner) is heavily invested in developing the algorithm
- **365 Retail Markets**: Uses human review overseas for ALL transactions — "passive AI" is marketing term
  - Transactions can queue overnight; manual team approves before settling
- **MicroMart**: Software makes call, human reviews and approves — similar to 365
- **HaHa**: Large overseas approval team makes transactions feel fast, but still human-reviewed
  - Testing true AI (SandStar-style) later in 2026
- **Implication for Kande VendTech**: SandStar gives strongest pitch for "automated" — genuine differentiator

### Machine Features
- Video boards are now standard across SandStar, HaHa, 365, MicroMart — no longer a differentiator
- SandStar: True AI = consistent, auditable, lower long-term cost
- 365: Mature platform, strong customer service, but technology-wise falling behind

## 3. SALES & LOCATION ACQUISITION

### Pop-In (Discovery) Strategy
- Discovery method: Show up unannounced with product samples to pitch on-the-spot
- Most effective when: Decision-maker is present; approach is friendly/non-salesy
- Bring top-performing snack samples — let the product speak
- Small wins (first pop-in successes) = momentum for next pitches

### Location Qualifying
- Key factors: Employee count, resident count (if apartments), foot traffic patterns
- Ask about current vending: Who is it with? Are they happy? When does contract expire?
- Understand property manager vs property owner distinction — pitch the right person

### MSA / National Account Sales (see also section 5)
- Target: Building management companies, facility managers, corporate campuses
- These are multi-site deals — one deal = multiple machines
- Decision cycle is longer but ROI is much higher per sales hour

## 4. OPERATIONS

### Inventory & Restocking
- Par levels: Establish minimum stock per SKU before triggering reorder
- New operators: Don't overstock — learn what your specific location buys before committing to large inventories
- Seasonal items: Pre-buy early to ensure supply; some items only made during season
- Testing: Want to test 2-3 cases of a new SKU? Call your opco and request specifically — don't rely on stock availability alone

### Delivery & Installation
- B-roll/how-to videos being created for SandStar and HaHa deliveries (documentation gap)
- When machine delivered: Document the entire unloading + setup process (community asset)
- Cantaloupe market setup, 365 setup, HaHa setup — each needs its own documentation

### Machine Maintenance
- Track cut/short orders systematically — report to opco regularly to build forecast
- Know your delivery driver — relationship = priority service

## 5. PRICING & MARGINS

### Pricing Philosophy (from VP Weeklies)
- MA deal passes through ALL Vistar discounts — no markup at MA level
- Your margin = product cost (at your tier discount) vs. vending price
- Don't price below sustainable margin — understand your full costs first
- Seasonal items: Buy at pre-buy price → sell at normal vending price = enhanced margin

### Discount Optimization
- Order size thresholds: Hit the minimum to unlock next tier (e.g., add $50 of product to get 2% off vs 1%)
- Combine pre-buy + volume discount on same order
- Establish demand forecast with opco → prevents cuts → stabilizes weekly costs
