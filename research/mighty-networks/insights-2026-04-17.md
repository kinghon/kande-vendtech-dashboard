## 1. Location Strategy

**Best-performing location types (ranked by revenue potential):**
1. **Hotels near airports** — operator reporting $6k/month single machine; "people will pay any price you set"
2. **Mid-rise luxury apartments (250–466 units)** — core target; ideal placement near gym + mailroom + lobby
3. **Government/municipal buildings** — City Hall, DPW breakrooms; steady foot traffic, less churn, longer contracts
4. **Healthcare** — VA hospital (2,500–3,000 daily), health offices, nursing homes, senior care; operators replacing Canteen
5. **Fitness centers / YMCA** — gym placements highly engaged customers
6. **Colleges/universities** — contact facilities manager, not the dean

**Key placement rules from operators:**
- Always place near gym + mailroom intersection — "natural placement zone"
- 24/7 access is critical; avoid locations with restricted hours unless adjacent to fitness center
- 133-unit buildings can work (~$1,500/mo), but 250+ units is the sweet spot
- Sister properties = land and expand; Willow Bridge is a chain worth pursuing

**Deal timelines (real data):**
- Fast deal: Pop-in March 2 → Contract signed March 22 = **20 days**
- Average: 2 months of consistent pop-ins to first location
- Long tail: Some operators chase a location for **2 years** before landing it (it paid off)
- 5 months back-and-forth for corporate approval at luxury complexes — normal

**Vegas-specific:**
- Operator posted needing help with an **Avenue5** property lead (Vegas-based) — Avenue5 manages many luxury complexes; good chain to pursue
- Machine near **The Sphere** area is already placed and doing well — tourist premium pricing
- Another VP actively seeking HAHA machines with white interior for a 3-location Vegas deal — active local market

**Watch out for:**
- **Lone Star Markets** using 5-year contracts to lock properties; already showing up in multiple markets. They use open-air markets, high theft risk. Counter with: AI machine, managed service, lower liability pitch
- **Canteen** service is poor everywhere — this is your opening pitch at locations with existing Canteen machines

- Doritos, Cheetos Puffs
- Smucker's Uncrustables (Grape/Wheat)

- Red Bull
- Dubai chocolates (from Faire) — trending hot, operators calling it a top seller
- Pressed smoothies (Costco Business Center): **buy $2.40, sell $5.50–$6.00** — exceptional margin
- Alani Nu (Lime Slush flavor popular; Sam's/Costco often out of stock — plan ahead)
- Lenny & Larry's cookies
- Tide Pods (small bags) — toiletry vending works in residential

**Pricing guidance:**
- Hotels: price high, no resistance — people pay $8 for a Diet Coke at the zoo; your $4 Coke is a deal
- General pricing: **$4 for candy bar or Coke is completely normal** — don't talk yourself out of it
- Smoothies and premium items can go $5.50–$7 depending on location
- Pricing ending in .95 vs .99 is debated — no consensus, test your location
- Subsidized pricing (employer pays 40%) is possible via most platforms — good for corporate/breakroom deals

**Seasonal adjustments:**
- Spring/Summer: hot dogs, buns, sunscreen, paper towels phasing in
- St. Patrick's Day: Gatorade, Gatorlyte, energy drinks, Tylenol spike 33%+ above normal
- Warm weather: stock chocolate carefully; plan cold-chain sourcing

**Sourcing:**
- **Vistar** (main distributor): get address verification early — takes 1–2 months; submit tax-exempt form
- **VendHub Marketplace** for machine-specific products
- **Costco Business Center** for smoothies, bulk items
- **Faire** for trending/specialty items (Dubai chocolate, boutique snacks)
- **Sam's Club / Costco** for everyday items; use CapitalOneShopping.com for cashback rewards
- USG rebate program: target 30% COGS after rebates; self-report for Hershey's and others

## 3. Operations

**Machine management:**
- VendHub is the core platform — use for orders, machine management, inventory
- HAHA app: use for promotions, planograms, reporting (limited monthly reports)
- VendInsights coming soon for cross-machine analytics
- AI automation: operators using Airtable + Slack for expiration date alerts (7-day, 3-day, day-of)
- BLUENOTE AI for field notetaking — free tier, integrates with CRMs

**Stocking & logistics:**
- Clax cart ($50–80) handles $400 restocks and folds flat for vehicle storage
- Husky 12-gallon bins (waterproof/dustproof/stackable) — perfect for 20oz bottles
- Bins pre-loaded at warehouse → wheel directly to machine
- Warehouse setup: push cart through pick lanes; organize by SKU category
- Portable freezer for ice cream transport in heat (Las Vegas critical — 100°F+ kills frozen product in car)

**Connectivity:**
- HAHA offline issues are common — Tenda router + SIM removal helps
- OptConnect: converts cellular to Ethernet for locations without WiFi
- HAHA server outages occur (April 2026 reported) — not operator error

**Financial systems:**
- Profit First recommended (multiple operators use it); reconciliation heavy at scale
- Relay Bank or Mercury for Profit First implementation (multiple accounts)
- Business credit card separate from personal — Relay or dedicated CC
- Workers comp for 1099 contractors is an open question — get a ghost policy

**Expiration tracking:**
- Health departments require logs for temperature-controlled food
- Some states require permit per location for fresh food
- Use a sticker system or app — inspectors in some markets want historical logs

## 4. Sales & Pop-Ins

**What's working:**
- **Gift basket** for first follow-up meeting — one operator swears by it
- **10-second opener** — the Module 5 framework: be clear, human, and lead with "no-cost amenity"
- **Discovery over pitching** — 4 core questions, listen like a partner
- **Augmented reality** — some operators using AR apps to show the machine in the space during pop-ins; converts skeptics
- **Pitch deck to video** — sending video instead of PDF via email; better engagement
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** — search by city + HQ + 50+ employees; gets office building leads fast
- **Social media** — one operator says it "moves the needle"; builds inbound leads

**Email pop-ins:**
- Subject line is the key variable — test aggressively
- One operator used direct cold email to Vistar's exec team about pricing → got a meeting; same approach works for property management chains
- Warm intro via friends at properties = fastest path to PM

**Objections:**
- "We already have a contract" → ask when it expires; plant the seed
- Canteen locations → lead with poor service angle; offer to do a no-obligation walkthrough
- No reference locations yet → reference Modern Amenities / VP community case studies

**Follow-up:**
- "When the answer isn't yes" = normal; long sales cycles are expected
- Follow-up philosophy: consistent, non-pushy, every 2–3 weeks
- One operator: when PM forwards flyer to Regional Manager, wait 5–7 days then follow up directly

## 7. Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Sandstar Taste Series locks** — multiple operators reporting trippable locks; Sandstar working on fix but not resolved. Avoid this model for high-traffic/low-supervision locations until fixed.
2. **WeVend contract fees** — $111–131/month before any product; read every line. Several operators caught off guard.
3. **Financing at 3% monthly** — one lender quoted this; that's 36% APR. Shop around.
4. **Stockwell theft visibility** — Stockwell 2.0 has weak AI for theft detection; operators getting beaten. Know this going in.
5. **HAHA to second floor** — elevator/stairs logistics are brutal with these machines; assess access before signing contract.
6. **Sandstar outdoors** — even covered outdoor placements causing issues; don't place outdoors.
7. **Vistar address verification** — submit immediately when starting; takes 1–2 months to clear.
8. **Fresh food without health permits** — some states require a permit per location; call your health department first.
9. **Chocolate in Vegas heat** — without cold-chain planning (portable freezer, insulated bags), stock melts in transit.
10. **Over-relying on surveys before launch** — "there's no perfect product list; start sensibly and let data guide you."
