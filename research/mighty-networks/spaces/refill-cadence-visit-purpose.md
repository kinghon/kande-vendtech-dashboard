Modern Amenities

Optional reads that sharpen judgment around when to visit, when to wait, and how to interpret messy real-world signals.

Cadence, Coverage & Capacity

What it is: Plain-language guidance on inventory cadence tradeoffs from a major retail advisory firm.
Why it helps vending: Frames frequency as a business choice, balancing labor, stockouts, and shelf constraints.
Link: https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/operations/our-insights/how-retailers-can-get-inventory-right

What it is: Practitioner-oriented discussion of capacity limits in small-format retail.
Why it helps vending: Reinforces why space—not demand alone—often determines refill urgency and assortment discipline.
Link: https://www.retailwire.com/discussion/how-much-inventory-is-too-much/

Demand Variability & Irregular Use

What it is: Short explainer on demand variability and uneven usage patterns in retail settings.
Why it helps vending: Normalizes lumpy sales days so operators don’t overreact to noise.
Link: https://hbr.org/2017/01/managing-demand-variability

What it is: Practical look at forecasting limits in real operations, not math-heavy models.
Why it helps vending: Encourages pattern recognition over precision when demand is inherently irregular.
Link: https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/why-forecasting-is-so-hard/

Visit Purpose & Field Prioritization

What it is: Field-service prioritization principles written for operators, not engineers.
Why it helps vending: Helps decide what actually warrants a visit versus what can wait.
Link: https://www.fieldserviceusa.com/issues/field-service-prioritization/

What it is: Overview of preventive versus reactive service in retail-adjacent environments.
Why it helps vending: Clarifies where proactive checks add value and where they just add miles.
Link: https://www.servicenow.com/workflows/field-service-management/preventive-maintenance.html

Signal vs Noise (Operational Judgment)

What it is: High-level discussion of separating meaningful signals from random variation.
Why it helps vending: Supports calmer decisions when data looks noisy week to week.
Link: https://fs.blog/noise/

Books 

What it is: The Goal — narrative introduction to flow and constraints.
Why it helps vending: Builds intuition for bottlenecks and visit priorities without formulas or jargon.
Link: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/193537/the-goal-by-eliyahu-m-goldratt/

What it is: Thinking in Bets — decision-making under uncertainty.
Why it helps vending: Encourages probabilistic thinking when refill timing and stockouts aren’t perfectly predictable.
Link: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/550835/thinking-in-bets-by-annie-duke/
