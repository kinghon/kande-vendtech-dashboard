# From Zero to $18,000/Month at ONE Hotel Location (Here's Exactly How He Did It)
**Video ID:** 8ih4aTXaot8
**Date transcribed:** 2026-03-26
**Source:** Vendingpreneurs YouTube

## Summary

Interview with Tom Canarino, a Vendingpreneurs member who came from a digital marketing agency background. He joined VP ~Jan 2025, got first locations within 60 days via pop-ins. Now has 17 machines (3 more on order), averaging $15-25K/month gross.

### The Hotel Banger Location

- **How he found it:** Sent a personal direct mailer that reached the right decision maker at a national franchise-owned hotel (400 beds, near a metro area)
- **Sales cycle:** ~1 year from first contact to signed deal
- **The journey:**
  1. Got meeting with Director of Operations — pitched as "local growing business"
  2. Hotel had existing self-managed micro market in lobby (theft issues, staff time-sink for checkout)
  3. Deal initially fell through — hotel wanted to keep micro market
  4. Owner visited, liked the idea — saw potential for freeing up front desk staff and improving guest satisfaction
  5. Deal signed end of 2025, machines installed early March 2026
- **Setup:** 4 machines installed (originally planned 3, glad he did 4)
- **Revenue:** Weekends averaging $1,000+/day across 4 machines. Weekdays $500-700/day. This is SLOW SEASON.
- **Projected:** ~$18K/month minimum in slow season. Could hit $25-30K in peak.
- **Commission:** Hotel takes 15% of gross, but all products are marked up for hotel environment
- **Perks:** Hotel gave him locked on-premise storage for restocking
- **Restocking:** Crew there every other day

### Key Insights for Kande VendTech

1. **Hotels are goldmine locations** — high foot traffic, captive audience, willingness to pay premium prices
2. **Direct mail CAN work** — but it's expensive and low ROI generally. This was a lucky hit that got to the right person
3. **Long sales cycles are worth it** — 1 year of follow-up yielded a potential $250K+/year location
4. **Replace existing micro markets** — hotels managing their own food service = pain point (theft, staff distraction, checkout lines)
5. **Mark up for environment** — hotel guests expect and accept premium pricing
6. **"Professional persistence"** — don't give up until you get a firm hard no
7. **Frame as partnership, not sales** — "looking for partnership opportunities" vs "selling"
8. **On-premise storage is a huge win** — negotiate for it, makes restocking much more efficient
9. **Smart machines > traditional** — Tom quickly transitioned from traditional to smart machines
10. **Student housing has seasonal eb/flow** — summer and holidays cause dips

### Applicable to Kande VendTech Strategy

- **Hotel prospecting:** This validates the hotel/hospitality vertical. Look for franchise-owned hotels where the owner/GM is accessible
- **Micro market replacement pitch:** "We replace your self-managed micro market — eliminates theft, frees up your staff, increases guest satisfaction, and costs you nothing"
- **Revenue share model:** 15% commission to property is a good benchmark for high-traffic hotel locations
- **Patience in enterprise sales:** Some of the best locations take 6-12 months to close
