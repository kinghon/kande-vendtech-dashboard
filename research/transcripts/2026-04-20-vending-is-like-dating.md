## Key Insights

- **Every location has its own product profile** — no two buildings are the same; each has its own preferences that need to be learned over time.
- **"It's like dating" is a client-facing frame** — a disarming, relatable analogy that sets realistic expectations with property managers about the ramp-up period.
- **Weeks 2–3 reveal your winners** — start with general variety, then use early sales data to double and triple down on what's moving.
- **Customization = retention** — when a location feels like you're dialing in their preferences, it builds loyalty and reduces churn risk.
- **Coke vs. Pepsi is real** — brand preference varies by building; let data drive SKU decisions, not gut feeling.
- **Kande takeaway:** Use the "dating" analogy during onboarding conversations with new location managers — it normalizes the 30–60 day learning curve and reduces pressure to have everything perfect at install.
