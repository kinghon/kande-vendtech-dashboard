# The Problem With Amazon FBA?
Source: https://youtu.be/7bv28mwOKmg | Scraped: 2026-05-11

Next in detail, we have Amazon FBA. This can work. It is very much a viable business model, and you're looking at a very low barrier of entry. $500 to $2,500 to launch at the bare minimum, which covers your seller account, initial inventory, and basic tools. You'll have tight margins and high per-unit costs at this level. More realistic launch budget for most new sellers is between $2,500 to $6,000. In fact, this is the most commonly cited range for a first product launch. This covers inventory sourcing, FBA fulfillment fees, Amazon's monthly subscription, packaging, and advertising. And it gets even more expensive if you're looking at a private label, a recommended budget of $20,000 to $30,000. There's also Amazon referral fees, usually around 50% of the selling price per item, and FBA fulfillment fees, monthly and long-term storage fees, EPC advertising. Amazon even charges low inventory fees for sellers who maintain insufficient stock level. And at top of everything else, you're platform dependent. Amazon controls everything, especially the fee structure, which they change regularly. You aren't in control and you're building on rented land.
