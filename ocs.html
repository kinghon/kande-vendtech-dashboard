📊 What is OCS?

Office Coffee Service means you install and maintain coffee equipment at client locations and deliver supplies (beans, pods, cups, cream, sugar) on a recurring route schedule. Same customer as vending, same visit, bigger contract.

FCV went from soda-only → vending → OCS → micro-markets → frictionless stores. OCS was a key step in their growth.

📈 Why add it now

  • Your vending accounts are already pre-qualified OCS customers
  • Upsell on same route — one driver, one invoice, higher contract value
  • OCS gross margins typically 40–60% on supplies
  • Recurring monthly revenue vs. per-transaction vending
  • FCV built a Las Vegas showroom specifically for coffee tastings — property managers drive to them

🎯 Best target accounts

  • Office buildings / corporate campuses — highest OCS spend per employee
  • Hotels (employee break rooms, not guest-facing)
  • Medical offices & clinics
  • Warehouses & distribution centers — high headcount, long shifts
  • Apartment complex leasing offices
  • Auto dealerships
Avoid: small offices under 10 people — margins too thin for route economics

🏆 FCV's OCS playbook (confirmed)

  • Built showroom at Las Vegas warehouse — hotel/restaurant managers come in for tastings
  • Converted Airstream into mobile showroom during COVID to go to clients
  • "Customers all week long coming in, we teach them everything from how machines work to running whole coffee houses"
  • OCS is a natural add-on to their vending/micro-market base

Equipment Partners

ENTRY LEVEL — Start here

Keurig Commercial

Single-serve pods. Lowest cost, easiest client approval, almost no maintenance. Become a licensed Keurig commercial partner. Machine cost $200–$500. Pods at wholesale 30–40% below retail. Best for small/mid-size offices.

VOLUME — Office standard

BUNN

Industry standard for high-volume drip coffee. Extremely reliable. Has a dealer/distributor program — Lighthouse Coffee Services is a BUNN distributor in LV. Purchase machines outright ($300–$600), high bean margins. Best for warehouses, large offices.

PREMIUM — Upsell tier

Franke / Jura

Bean-to-cup espresso machines. Clients pay $200–$400/month. Target law firms, medical practices, hotels, boutique offices. Higher margin per account but more service complexity. Add after you have 20+ standard accounts running.

COLD BREW / SPECIALTY

Nitro Cold Brew Tap

Growing fast in tech offices and gyms. Monthly keg delivery model. Differentiator FCV can't match at scale. Niche but high-margin for the right accounts.

Launch Playbook

1

Start with existing accounts

Your vending clients are pre-qualified OCS customers. Walk your top 20 A/B accounts and ask: "Would you like to add coffee service to your break room? Same visit, one invoice." No new sales effort needed to start.

2

Pick your entry equipment

Start with Keurig commercial pods. $300–$500 machine cost. Purchase 5–10 machines. Lowest learning curve, easiest client approval, minimal service calls. Don't buy bean-to-cup until you have 20+ pod accounts running.

3

Set up local supply relationship

Contact VESTA Coffee Roasters and Dark Moon for wholesale pricing. Get pods (Keurig-compatible) from a broadline distributor. Build a standard supply kit: coffee, tea, creamer, sugar packets, cups, stirrers. Price the kit at 40–60% margin.

4

Set pricing and pitch

Start at $75/month for small offices, $5–$8/employee for larger ones. Include machine, biweekly delivery of supplies, and all consumables. Pitch it as "we already service your vending — let us handle your coffee too, zero additional vendors for you."

5

Copy FCV's showroom idea

Your warehouse at 5725 S Valley View can have a simple coffee station. When property managers come in for any reason, offer a tasting. "We have 3 different roasts — want to try before you commit?" Closes faster than any brochure. FCV gets "customers all week long" from this.

6

Track separately in CRM

Add an OCS flag to accounts so you know which locations have vending-only vs. vending+OCS. This lets you target upsell outreach and track OCS revenue separately.

7

Scale with BUNN and bean-to-cup after 20 accounts

Once you have 20+ Keurig accounts and the route is running smoothly, add BUNN drip for high-volume locations and Franke/Jura bean-to-cup for premium upsells. At 50 accounts you're looking at $120k/year in OCS revenue with existing staff.
