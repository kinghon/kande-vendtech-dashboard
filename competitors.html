Signs a Property is Unhappy

Visual Signs

  • Machine looks old, dirty, or damaged
  • "Out of Order" sign on machine
  • Half-empty product selection
  • No contactless payment reader
  • Handwritten "cash only" sign

What Staff Says

  • "It's always broken"
  • "Takes forever to get restocked"
  • "We've complained but nothing changes"
  • "The selection is terrible"
  • "I don't even know who our vendor is"

Online Indicators

  • Property reviews mention vending complaints
  • Current vendor has poor Google reviews
  • No vendor website/social presence
  • Vendor hasn't updated Google profile

Questions to Uncover Pain Points

  • "Do you have vending service currently?"
  • "How's that working for you?"
  • "When's the last time you saw the vendor?"
  • "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"

Service Quality

  • "How quickly do they respond when something breaks?"
  • "Do they have a direct number for you?"
  • "Are residents/staff happy with the selection?"
  • "How often is the machine fully stocked?"

Contract Situation

  • "When does it come up for renewal?"
  • "Do you have an exclusivity clause?"
  • "What would make you consider switching?"

Displacement Sequence

Step 1: Research

  • Google the property ‚Äî check for vending complaints in reviews
  • Identify current vendor (signage on machine)
  • Check vendor's Google reviews
  • Look for contract end dates (ask during pop-in)

Step 2: Pop-In

  • Bring gift basket ($5 from Costco)
  • Ask the 3 questions: Have vending? Like it? Change anything?
  • Take photos of current machine (secretly)
  • Get decision-maker name + contact

Step 3: Follow Up

  • Send comparison email (you vs. current vendor)
  • Include their current vendor's bad reviews
  • Offer no-risk trial
  • Propose site visit to show your machine

Step 4: Close

  • Bring proposal PDF (professional)
  • Handle contract concerns (most are month-to-month)
  • Offer to coordinate removal with old vendor
  • Set installation date before leaving
