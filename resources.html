üìñ Key Terminology Glossary
Pop-In
An unannounced in-person visit to a prospect. The #1 way to close deals. Bring a gift basket!
Smart Market
What we call vending machines. NEVER say "vending machine" to prospects ‚Äî it sounds outdated.
Rev Share
Revenue sharing with property. Typically 3-5% starting month 6. Use as a closing incentive.
COGS
Cost of Goods Sold. Target 33% (3x markup). Track this religiously.
Touch
Any contact with a prospect (call, email, pop-in). Takes 8-10 touches to close.
Pull Threshold
If a machine does <$800/month after 90 days, PULL IT. Don't let pride keep bad placements.
NetVendor
Vendor certification required by Greystar and other large property managers. Get certified ASAP.
Planogram
The layout of products in a machine. Optimize based on sales data and property type.
Route Efficiency
Target 70% servicing, 30% driving. Cluster locations geographically.

The Kande VendTech philosophy: We're not selling vending machines ‚Äî we're providing a premium amenity that increases property value and tenant satisfaction.

Core Principles

  • Local advantage: "I'm local. You can call me." ‚Äî This beats national operators every time.
  • Relationship first: Don't pitch on the first visit. Build rapport, drop a gift basket, learn their needs.
  • Persistence wins: 8-10 touches to close. Most give up after 2. Don't be most people.
  • Quality over quantity: One great location beats ten mediocre ones.
üí° Pro Tip: Property managers get 10+ smart market pitches per month. Stand out by showing up in person with value (gift basket, industry insights) ‚Äî not just another sales email.

Language Matters

  • ‚ùå "Vending machine" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Smart market" or "refreshment amenity"
  • ‚ùå "Can I place a machine?" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Would your residents benefit from 24/7 refreshment access?"
  • ‚ùå "It's free" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Zero cost to you, plus revenue share after month 6"

Pop-ins are responsible for 90% of successful placements. Master this skill.

Before You Go

  • Research the property (units, reviews, existing amenities)
  • Prepare a gift basket ($5 from Costco ‚Äî snacks + business card)
  • Have your proposal PDF ready on your phone
  • Dress smart casual ‚Äî you're a business owner, not a salesman

The Three Questions

  1. "Do you currently have a vending or refreshment option for residents?"
  2. "How's that working out? Are residents happy with it?"
  3. "If you could change anything about it, what would that be?"

Handling Gatekeepers

  • Always be friendly to front desk staff ‚Äî they control access
  • Ask for the property manager BY NAME if you have it
  • If PM is busy: "No problem! Could I leave this gift basket for [name]?"
  • Always get a business card or name for follow-up
‚úÖ Success Script: "Hi! I'm Kurtis with Kande VendTech ‚Äî we're a local Las Vegas company that provides smart refreshment solutions for luxury apartments. I was in the area and wanted to introduce myself. Is [PM name] available for just a quick hello?"

After the Visit

  • Log the visit in CRM immediately (notes, outcome, next action)
  • Send a follow-up email within 24 hours
  • Schedule your next touch (call in 3-5 days if interested)

Cold calls work best as follow-ups to pop-ins, not first contact. But sometimes you need to call first.

Best Times to Call

  • Tuesday‚ÄìThursday, 10am‚Äì11:30am or 2pm‚Äì4pm
  • Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons
  • Property managers are often in meetings before 10am

Opening Script

"Hi [Name], this is Kurtis with Kande VendTech. We're a local Las Vegas company that helps luxury apartments enhance their amenity offerings with smart refreshment solutions. I noticed [property name] and thought your residents might benefit. Do you have 2 minutes for a quick question?"

If They Say "Send Info"

  • Get their email and send immediately
  • Ask: "What specifically would you like me to include?"
  • Schedule a call: "Great, I'll send that now. Can I follow up Thursday to answer any questions?"
‚ö†Ô∏è Don't: Read from a script robotically. Sound like every other vendor. Push too hard on the first call. Forget to log the call in CRM.

Email is the least effective first-touch method, but essential for follow-ups and sequences.

Subject Lines That Work

  • "Quick question about [Property Name]"
  • "Resident amenity idea for [Property Name]"
  • "Following up from my visit Tuesday"
  • "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"

Email Structure

  1. Hook: Reference something specific about them/property
  2. Value: One clear benefit (not features)
  3. Proof: Quick stat or social proof
  4. CTA: One simple ask (call, meeting, reply)
üí° Use the Email Templates page: Pre-built sequences for different scenarios ‚Äî just customize and send.

Apollo Sequences

Set up multi-step sequences that automatically follow up. Typical sequence:

  • Day 1: Initial email
  • Day 3: Follow-up if no reply
  • Day 7: Value-add email (case study, stat)
  • Day 14: Final "closing the loop" email

Gift baskets are the #1 differentiator in vending sales. They work because nobody else does this.

What to Include ($5-10 budget)

  • 2-3 premium snacks (Kind bars, Skinny Pop, quality chocolate)
  • 1-2 beverages (sparkling water, premium soda)
  • Your business card
  • Small branded item if you have one
  • Cellophane wrap with ribbon ‚Äî presentation matters!

Where to Buy

  • Costco Business Center ‚Äî Best bulk pricing
  • Sam's Club ‚Äî Good alternative
  • Dollar Tree ‚Äî Baskets, cellophane, ribbons

When to Use

  • ‚úÖ First pop-in (always)
  • ‚úÖ After getting a meeting scheduled
  • ‚úÖ After they sign (bigger basket)
  • ‚úÖ Holiday drop-offs to existing clients
  • ‚ùå With a cold email (they can't eat a PDF)
‚úÖ ROI: $5 basket ‚Üí one placement ‚Üí $24,000+/year revenue. Best investment in your business.

You've built the relationship. Now it's time to ask for the business.

Trial Close Questions

  • "If we could address [concern], would you be ready to move forward?"
  • "On a scale of 1-10, how interested are you? What would make it a 10?"
  • "What would need to happen for this to be a yes?"

The Assumptive Close

Once they're interested, assume the sale:

  • "Great! I'll have the agreement ready by Friday. Should I email or bring it in person?"
  • "Perfect. Let's look at installation timing ‚Äî does next week work?"

Referral Ask (Critical!)

"Thank you so much! Quick question ‚Äî do you know 2-3 other property managers who might benefit from this? If you introduce me, I'll start your revenue share immediately instead of waiting until month 6."

üí° Timing: Ask for referrals RIGHT after they sign, when excitement is highest.

Objections aren't rejections ‚Äî they're requests for more information.

"We already have vending"

Response: "That's great! How's the current service? [Pause] Most properties I work with had vending before and switched because of [common complaint]. Would you be open to seeing what's different about our smart market approach?"

"I need to check with corporate"

Response: "Absolutely understand. What information would help you make the case? I can put together a one-pager specifically for regional review. Who would be the decision-maker I should address it to?"

"We don't have space"

Response: "Our machines are quite compact ‚Äî about the size of a standard refrigerator. Many properties put them in the fitness center, mailroom, or near the pool. Could I take a quick look to see if there's a creative solution?"

"Send me information"

Response: "Happy to! What specifically would be most helpful ‚Äî pricing, the resident satisfaction data, or success stories from similar properties? And when would be a good time to follow up ‚Äî Thursday or Friday?"

"Not interested"

Response: "No problem at all. Mind if I ask ‚Äî is it the timing, the service concept, or something else? I want to make sure I'm not missing something that could actually help your residents."

‚ö†Ô∏è Never argue. Acknowledge their concern, ask a question, provide value. If they're firm, thank them and move on ‚Äî but add them to your 6-month follow-up list.

üè¢ Business Questions
How long does it take to close a deal?
Typically 10-17+ weeks from first contact to signed contract. It takes 8-10 touches, so start the clock early and stay consistent.
What's a good monthly revenue target per machine?
Minimum $2,000/month for it to be worthwhile. Great locations do $3,000-5,000+. If under $800/month after 90 days, pull the machine.
What locations should I avoid?
Gyms (especially bodybuilding), car dealerships, small medical offices, offices under 50 employees, the Las Vegas Strip (monopolized), and big retail chain breakrooms.
How much revenue share should I offer?
3-5% of gross revenue, starting month 6 (not immediately). Use as a closing incentive, not a default offer.
When should I hire help?
Around 12+ machines, you need help restocking. Budget $20/hour for restock drivers. Buy a van around 15 machines.
üíª Dashboard Questions
How do I import leads in bulk?
Go to Sales ‚Üí Lead Import. You can paste CSV data or upload a file. The system will dedupe and geocode automatically.
Where do I log a pop-in visit?
In the CRM, find the prospect and click "Log Activity." Select "Pop-In" as the type, add notes about the conversation, and set your next action.
How does the pipeline work?
Pipeline Board shows prospects as cards in stages. Drag cards between stages as deals progress. Each stage has automated task suggestions.
Can I generate contracts automatically?
Yes! Use Sales ‚Üí Contract Generator. Fill in the property details and it creates a professional PDF ready for signature.
How do I track follow-ups?
The Follow-Ups page shows all scheduled next actions. You can also see overdue items on your Dashboard and Daily Planner.
‚öôÔ∏è Process Questions
What licenses do I need in Nevada?
Henderson business license, Clark County business license (for locations outside Henderson), SNHD health permit, and sales tax permit. Consider NetVendor certification for Greystar properties.
What's the best machine to buy?
SandStar AI Smart Cooler at $3,600 is the community favorite. $65/mo SaaS, 5-year warranty, AMEX accepted. Consider eVending or HaHa financing.
How do I handle spoilage?
Budget 2% of COGS for spoilage. Track expiration dates in inventory. Rotate stock (FIFO). Remove slow sellers after 4 weeks.
What payment processing should I use?
WeVend/Preva saves ~$1,656/year vs Stripe. ~5.5-5.9% flat rate, no per-transaction fee. Set up before machines ship.
