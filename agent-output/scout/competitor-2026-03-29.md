## 🔍 Competitor Review Check

### Canteen (Las Vegas — 1509 Western Ave, (702) 387-8789)
- **Yelp Aggregate (national):** 1.4/5 across 112 reviews at 35 US locations — consistently poor.
- Common complaint themes (national pattern, applies to LV): machines frequently empty/out of stock, poor responsiveness, pricing discrepancies (cash vs. card), slow service on reported issues.
- **Las Vegas specific:** No new notable LV-specific complaints surfaced this week, but the national 1.4/5 average is a damning baseline.
- **Opportunity:** Canteen's brand reputation is terrible. Any prospect that has Canteen can be pitched with "we're local, we respond fast, and we actually keep machines stocked."

### Five Star Vending (Las Vegas)
- **Website positioning:** Remote tracking technology for inventory, 24-hour response guarantee on machine malfunctions.
- **Employee reviews (Indeed):** Mixed — route drivers describe long days, lots of lifting, difficult building access. Management issues noted.
- No customer-facing complaints found this week. Not a big Google/Yelp presence in LV specifically.
- **Assessment:** Operationally adequate but not differentiated on quality or tech. Response guarantee mirrors KVT's offering.

### First Class Vending (Las Vegas / LA)
- **Trustindex:** Employee complaint from 2024 — "Bad management," "learned nothing," "don't take care of employees." Company response was generic/defensive.
- **Glassdoor:** 13 reviews, mixed. Route-based model, similar to Five Star.
- **Client-facing reviews:** Minimal public-facing client reviews found — low visibility, which also means low brand strength in LV.
- **Website:** Emphasizes micro-markets with fresh food, employer-paid model (no cost to host). Directly competing on the same pitch as KVT.
- **Assessment:** Operationally similar pitch to KVT (no cost, we handle everything, fresh food). Weakness: employee instability = service inconsistency.
