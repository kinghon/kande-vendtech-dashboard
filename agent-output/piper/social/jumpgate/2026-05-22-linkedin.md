## Post

"Build or buy AI?"

Most people ask this like it's a budget question. It's not.

We've helped businesses do both. Here's the decision we've seen go wrong most often in each direction:

**When companies buy and regret it:**
They bought a point solution (a chatbot, a scheduling tool, an "AI assistant") that works great in demos and breaks in production the moment their data looks different than the vendor's template. They're now locked into a monthly contract for a tool that doesn't actually automate anything — it just adds a UI on top of the work they were already doing.

**When companies build and regret it:**
They hired an AI consultant, spent three months building a custom agent, and the agent does one thing brilliantly but nothing else. It doesn't hand off to their CRM. It doesn't know what to do when the edge case hits. It needs a technical babysitter to keep it running.

The right question isn't build or buy. It's:

→ Do you have a process that's documented, repeatable, and producing consistent inputs?
→ Is that process connected to the other systems that need to act on its output?
→ Does your team understand what the AI is doing — and when to step in?

If yes to all three: you're ready to automate it, either way.

If no to any of them: fix the process first. AI won't fix it for you.

We help SMBs figure out which path fits — and either build the right thing or help them choose the right vendor. The honest answer is usually somewhere in between.

What's the build-vs-buy decision you're staring down right now?
