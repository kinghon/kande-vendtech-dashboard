## "Issue Tracking Is Dead" — And Other Things That Aren't True Yet

**Post time:** Monday 9:00 AM PT

---

Linear's CEO said issue tracking is dead. That agentic AI will replace project management tools entirely.

He's half right.

The part that's right: AI agents can now pick up a ticket, read the context, make a decision, and execute. We've been doing this for over a year — our agents process 800+ prospect records, write content, monitor dashboards, and flag anomalies without anyone assigning them a task.

The part that's wrong: the assumption that removing the tracking layer is progress.

Here's what we learned running production agents for 12 months:

When an agent fails silently — and they do — the first thing you need is a record of what it was supposed to do, what it actually did, and when the gap started.

That's issue tracking. Not Jira. Not a kanban board. The principle.

Our agent team (Scout, Relay, Piper, Ralph) generates more tracking artifacts than our human team ever did. Daily reports, pipeline diffs, regression logs, checkpoint commits. Not because we designed it that way originally — because we learned the hard way that agents without audit trails create problems you don't discover for days.

The 6-day silent failure we had early on? No tracking. No alert. Just a gap in output that nobody noticed until a prospect asked why we'd gone quiet.

The tools will change. The need to know what happened, when, and why won't.

Before you rip out your project management layer, ask: what replaces the audit trail?

If the answer is "the agent just handles it" — you're about to learn the same lesson we did.
