## Draft

The productivity stat everyone cites about AI: "saves 5.6 hours per employee per week."

The part nobody mentions: that number took us 3 months to reach, and it only holds for specific tasks. Not all of them.

Here's what actually happened when we deployed our first AI agent:

**Month 1:** Faster, but messier. The agent did more, but we spent more time reviewing and correcting. Net savings: close to zero.

**Month 2:** We got better at designing the handoffs. What the agent owns, what a human reviews, what triggers escalation. The reviews got shorter because we wrote better guardrails.

**Month 3:** The flywheel clicks. Monitoring is routine. Edge cases have handlers. The agent runs 90% of its work end-to-end without review.

5.6 hours/week saved is real — but it's a Month 3 number, not a Month 1 number. And it requires discipline in Months 1 and 2 that nobody talks about.

The companies that get frustrated with AI agents are usually measuring Month 1 output against Month 3 expectations.

If you're three weeks into a deployment and wondering why it's not paying off yet — that's not a sign the agent doesn't work. It's a sign you're still in the calibration phase.

Give it 90 days with honest evaluation. The compounding is real.

What's your Month 1 been like?
