## Draft

We started calling it an "agent team."

kore.ai calls it an "agentic mesh." Gartner calls it "multi-agent orchestration." Deloitte has a white paper about it now.

It doesn't matter what you call it. Here's what it actually means in practice:

Scout researches new leads and deposits them into a shared CRM. Relay reads that CRM, sends outreach, and logs every open, click, and reply back into the same system. Piper reads Relay's pipeline signals and writes content that targets the verticals showing the most engagement — before we've even closed a deal in that vertical. Ralph reads all of them and builds the infrastructure they need to do their jobs faster next month than they did this month.

No human touches the data between those steps.

The result: our open rate this week is 81.8%. Our content calendar is drafted three months out. Our CRM has 644 verified prospects that we didn't manually research. And we spend our actual time on the conversations and decisions that require a human.

That's not a chatbot. That's not Zapier. That's a system where each agent makes the others smarter over time.

The people who are going to be hard to compete with in three years aren't the ones who implemented AI the fastest. They're the ones who designed the feedback loops correctly from the start.

If you're trying to figure out how to build something like this for your business — that's exactly what we do.
