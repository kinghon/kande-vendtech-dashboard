## Draft: "The automation that saved us the most time wasn't what we expected."

We built five AI agents. A research agent, a sales agent, a follow-up agent, a content agent, a build agent.

We assumed the research agent would be the biggest time saver. The one that scours public records, finds new leads, profiles properties before anyone else calls — it felt like the high-value play.

It's not. Not in terms of hours returned.

The follow-up agent is.

The research agent replaces hours we'd spend on manual prospecting. But humans don't do prospecting consistently enough to feel the difference right away.

The follow-up agent replaces something humans are actually terrible at: staying in contact with 500+ leads at exactly the right cadence, without letting anything fall through. No "I forgot to follow up." No "I thought someone else was handling it." No "I'll send it Monday" that becomes two weeks later.

It's not glamorous work. It's the kind of work that kills momentum when it doesn't happen.

We're now at 564 prospects, 79% email open rate, 16% reply rate — from consistent follow-up. The kind of consistency that's boring to think about and hard to maintain without automation.

If you're deciding where to start with AI automation: start with the thing your team is most likely to let slip.

What's the task at your company that always falls through the cracks?
