Harvard Business Review published a piece this week with advice that sounds obvious: "To scale AI agents successfully, think of them like team members."

They're right. And most businesses get it exactly wrong.

Thinking of an agent like a team member doesn't mean giving it a name and a personality. It means:

**Onboarding it properly.** What does it know? What's it allowed to do? What does it escalate and to whom? If you can't answer those questions on day one, you haven't onboarded it — you've unleashed it.

**Holding it to performance reviews.** BCG found 74% of companies struggle to scale agents — not because the AI is bad, but because nobody measures it after launch. A team member who never gets feedback never improves. Neither does an agent.

**Giving it clear handoff protocols.** The moment an agent touches something it shouldn't, or drops a task without flagging it, is the moment trust breaks. That's not a model problem. It's a workflow design problem.

The companies getting real results from agents are treating them like junior hires — useful, capable, but requiring structure and oversight.

The ones struggling are treating them like magic buttons.
