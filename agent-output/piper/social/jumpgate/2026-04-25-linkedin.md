## Post

Scout's morning report came back this week with 2 new leads.

Six months ago it was 10. Three months ago it was 6.

The Las Vegas metro is basically mapped now — 601 qualified properties in the CRM, every major apartment complex, clinic, distribution center, and coworking space we targeted has been found, contacted, and tracked.

We built an AI research agent that did what it was supposed to do. And then we ran into the problem that AI can't solve.

The pipeline data: 601 contacts, 79% open rate, 16% reply rate. One deal in negotiating.

The gap isn't leads. The gap is conversion — specifically, the moment the automation stops and a human needs to step in with context, judgment, and a phone call. We underbuilt that layer. We spent six months on research and outreach agents and about six weeks on handoff design.

The warm leads don't go cold because the AI failed. They go cold because nobody built the bridge between "this prospect opened the email 14 times and replied twice" and "call them today."

That's what the next version of the system has to solve.

The post we published this week walks through what changes when you've found everyone — and what to build differently if you're starting an AI outreach system from scratch.

What's your conversion bottleneck right now — finding leads, or closing the ones you've already found?
