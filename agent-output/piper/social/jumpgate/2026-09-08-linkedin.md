## Draft

Gumloop raised $50M to make AI agents accessible to everyone — no code, no engineers, just drag-and-drop workflows.

That's not a criticism. It's a real product solving a real problem. If your workflow is predictable, structured, and repeatable, a no-code tool might be exactly right.

Here's where it breaks:

**When your data doesn't match their template.** No-code agents assume clean, consistent inputs. Real business data is messy, inconsistently formatted, and occasionally wrong. Custom systems handle this; no-code platforms surface it as errors.

**When you need memory across steps.** Most no-code tools run workflows as isolated events. If step 3 needs to know what happened in step 1 last week, you're now engineering around the platform's limitations — which defeats the point.

**When the agent needs judgment.** A lead replies with a question that doesn't fit any branch logic. A document arrives in an unexpected format. A task fails silently. No-code platforms weren't designed for these edge cases. They were designed for the clean 80%.

We build the other 20% — the systems that handle what no-code can't, talk to each other, and improve over time through feedback loops.

If you're hitting the ceiling of your current automation setup, that's the problem we work on.
