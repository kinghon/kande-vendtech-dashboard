## Post

We have 576 prospects in our pipeline.

75% of them opened at least one email. 28 have received proposals. The AI agents — Scout for research, Relay for outreach — ran almost entirely unsupervised and generated the best top-of-funnel metrics we've seen in two years of sales work.

And then there's the last 10 feet.

Jade Apartments has opened our contract 56 times. They just haven't signed it. That part — the follow-up call, the "is something holding you back?" conversation — can't be automated. Not because the technology doesn't exist, but because the prospect deserves a human at the finish line.

Here's what we've actually learned after 90 days of production AI:

**What compounded:** Consistent outreach at scale. Our AI agents never forget a follow-up, never miss a morning run, never get tired on Thursday afternoons. At 500+ prospects, that consistency is worth more than any individual smart reply.

**What plateaued:** Anything requiring judgment about a specific relationship. Which leads deserve a phone call vs. an email. When a prospect is stalling vs. genuinely not interested. What to say when someone's been sitting on a contract for six weeks.

The AI handles the volume. Humans handle the judgment calls.

If you're building an AI automation system and assuming it'll close deals autonomously — budget for the handoff. That's where the real work is.

What's the hardest part of your sales handoff? Would love to hear how others are thinking about this.
