## Draft (Company voice, ~260 words)

Las Vegas just ran the Clark County Spring Job Fair. Hundreds of companies showed up to hire — manufacturers, hotels, healthcare centers, distribution operations. It's real growth.

But most of those same companies are running their back-office operations the same way they did in 2018.

The hiring surge and the operations lag are two sides of the same coin. You can scale headcount, but if your intake workflows, follow-up sequences, and reporting still run on spreadsheets and someone's institutional memory, you're adding staff to manage problems that don't need human hands.

Here's what we've seen in the businesses we work with:

**The work that actually scales well with AI agents:**
- Follow-up sequences with clear triggers and defined outcomes
- Data aggregation across sources that don't talk to each other
- Intake routing that follows consistent rules (not judgment calls)
- Status updates that require pulling from 3 systems to construct a sentence

**The work that doesn't:**
- Relationship decisions (who gets the deal, who gets the call back first)
- Novel problem-solving (the edge case nobody wrote a rule for)
- Anything where the "right" answer depends on context a machine can't read

The businesses doing this well aren't the ones with the largest AI budgets. They're the ones that picked one workflow that consumed disproportionate time, built a focused solution, measured the result, and then extended from there. (AI Journal had a piece on this recently that nailed it.)

If you're in a hiring surge and your ops aren't keeping up — that gap is worth looking at.

What's the one workflow in your business that you wish just ran itself?
