## Post

Most people think AI readiness is about having the right data.

It's not. It's about having the right process.

Before we build anything for a new client, we run a three-question test:

1. Can you describe this process in writing — specifically enough that a new hire could follow it on day one?
2. Do the inputs arrive in a consistent, structured format at least 90% of the time?
3. Can you define "working correctly" in a way you could evaluate objectively?

If all three are yes: we're ready to build. Usually takes days, not months.

If one is no: that's the first project. Not the AI layer — the process definition work.

The businesses that get the fastest ROI from agent work are almost never the ones with the most sophisticated processes. They're the ones with the most clearly documented processes. Boring SOPs outperform interesting ideas every time.

We've seen this from the other side too. Our own vending outreach pipeline — 591 contacts, 79% open rate, 5 agents — was built on a spec we spent two days writing before touching a single API. The spec was the hard part.

If you're trying to figure out where to start with AI automation: start with the thing you can document completely. Not the most exciting thing. The most documented thing.
