## LinkedIn Post

There's a thing that happens with AI automation projects that nobody talks about until it's too late.

You pick a platform. You build workflows on it. Six months in, the platform gets acquired, the pricing doubles, or the feature you're dependent on disappears in a "product focus" announcement.

Now you have two options:
1. Rebuild everything from scratch
2. Stay locked in and pay whatever they ask

This isn't hypothetical. It's been happening steadily as the enterprise AI consolidation continues. Snowflake + OpenAI. BCG + Anthropic. The big players are absorbing the platforms SMBs depend on, and the roadmaps are shifting toward enterprise features and enterprise pricing.

The businesses at risk aren't the ones who picked the wrong tool. They're the ones who built *inside* a tool instead of *on top of* their own logic.

Here's the practical difference:

**Inside a tool:** Your workflows live in their interface. Your prompts are stored in their system. Your data structures match their schema. If they go away, so does your automation.

**On top of your own logic:** Your processes are documented and portable. The AI system is a layer, not the foundation. Switching providers means reconfiguring outputs, not rebuilding the entire operation.

We learned this building our own agent team. Scout, Relay, Piper, Ralph — each agent has a clear interface contract. When we've swapped models or updated providers, the downstream system didn't care. The logic stayed ours.

That's not an accident. It's a design choice that takes longer upfront and protects you indefinitely.

If you're evaluating AI automation vendors right now, ask one question: *if this provider disappeared tomorrow, how long would it take to rebuild?*

If the answer is "months," you don't have automation. You have a dependency.
