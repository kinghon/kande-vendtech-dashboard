## Post

For the first six months, everything in our AI pipeline was outbound.

Scout found the leads. Relay sent the emails. We tracked opens, measured replies, refined the message. All push.

This week, two inbound partnership inquiries landed in the CRM from separate sources. Both came in within 24 hours.

It's a small thing. But it's also not.

Here's what the shift from outbound to inbound actually looks like in practice: you don't flip a switch. The pipeline runs long enough, the emails are specific enough, the content is credible enough — and eventually someone reaches out to you first.

Consistency at scale is what produces that. Not smarter targeting. Not a better subject line. Just showing up 582 times, adjusting the message, and not stopping.

The temptation when building an AI outreach system is to measure it in days. The signal is in months.

What's your ratio of inbound to outbound right now — and how long did it take to shift?
