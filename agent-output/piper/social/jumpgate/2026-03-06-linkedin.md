## Draft

We've been running an AI agent system in production for about 4 months now.

Here's what actually moved the needle — and what we built that felt impressive but didn't matter much.

**The stuff that worked:**

The outreach agent. We went from "who should we reach out to this week?" being a manual research question that took hours — to a daily brief of 5–6 qualified prospects with contact info, property details, and outreach timing. The consistency alone was worth it. When humans do research, they do a lot one week and skip it the next. Agents don't have off weeks.

The follow-up agent. 75% open rate across 550+ prospects. That number isn't magic — it's what happens when follow-ups happen at the right interval, every time, without someone having to remember to do it.

**The stuff that didn't move the needle the way we expected:**

The "smart routing" logic we built to auto-prioritize leads. In theory, a scoring algorithm should surface the hottest prospects automatically. In practice, a 12-open / 3-click signal is obvious to a human in 2 seconds. The algorithm mostly confirmed what was already intuitive. Worth building for scale — not the productivity win we thought it would be at our current volume.

**The lesson:**

The ROI of AI isn't usually "look what AI can do." It's "look what we can do consistently now that we couldn't before." Consistency at scale — the kind humans are actually bad at — is where the real returns are.

What's a workflow in your business that breaks down because consistency is hard to maintain? Genuinely curious.
