There's a phrase I keep hearing from operators right now: "We can't turn it off."

That's the transition nobody talks about — from "we're experimenting with AI" to "our business depends on this running."

WBN Digital called it last month: AI is shifting from optional tools to mission-critical systems. And that shift is happening faster than most small businesses expected.

Here's the problem: the infrastructure decisions you make while AI is optional are not the same decisions you'd make when it becomes essential.

When we built Scout and Relay — our outreach and follow-up agents — we made some of those decisions wrong. The agents worked fine in testing. In production, over months, the failure modes looked completely different:

- Silent errors that didn't surface until the 90-day recalibration
- Handoff gaps where no agent knew they were the responsible party
- A 6-day blackout we didn't catch until a prospect slipped through

We didn't plan for any of it. Because we built like it was optional.

If you're in the "still experimenting" phase, you have a window. The architecture choices you make now — ownership, observability, handoff design — are the ones you'll be locked into when the system is mission-critical.

What does your AI stack look like if it's unavailable for 6 hours? 6 days?

If you don't have a clear answer, that's the first thing to figure out.
