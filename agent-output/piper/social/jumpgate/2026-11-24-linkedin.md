The first year of running AI agents in production is about survival.

Silent failures. Handoff gaps. That moment when an agent confidently does the wrong thing for six days and nobody notices.

The second year is harder.

Not because the system breaks more — it breaks less. The hard part is that everyone (including you) starts treating the system as reliable. And reliable systems get less attention. Less monitoring. Fewer check-ins.

That's when calibration decay sets in.

Our outreach system is now at 84.5% open rate across 815+ prospects. The agents are better than they were 12 months ago at every measurable task. But the workflows those agents serve? The market shifted. The prospects changed. What worked in month 3 doesn't work in month 14.

Three things we learned about year two:

1. **Recalibration isn't optional.** Every 90 days, we review what the agents are doing — not whether they're doing it correctly, but whether the *thing itself* is still the right thing to do.

2. **Success metrics drift.** Open rates went up. But the deals that close don't always come from the highest-engagement prospects. The metric that matters in year one isn't necessarily the metric that matters in year two.

3. **The hardest decision is what to stop.** Agents don't get tired. They'll keep running a workflow forever. Knowing when to shut something down requires a human who understands the business, not just the system.

If you're building AI agents for your business, plan for year two before year one is over.
