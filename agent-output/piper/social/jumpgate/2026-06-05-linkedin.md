## Post

A company called Every.io launched AI-native back-office agents for startups back in March. Banking. Invoicing. Compliance. All handled by agents, no human touchpoints.

It made a splash. And rightfully so — this kind of thing would have been science fiction three years ago.

But here's the thing we keep seeing play out with off-the-shelf agent platforms: they work beautifully until your data doesn't look like what they expected.

Every.io built a platform around a model of how startups handle their back office. If your invoicing flow, your chart of accounts, your compliance structure, and your data pipeline all look like that model, it's genuinely great. Fast to implement. No engineering required.

If they don't — and for most businesses with real operational complexity, they won't — you end up with an agent that automates the 60% of the workflow that was already simple, and leaves the 40% that was actually consuming your team's time completely untouched.

We've been building custom AI systems for small and mid-sized businesses for over a year now. The pattern is consistent: the workflows that would benefit most from automation are also the ones that are too idiosyncratic for a general-purpose platform to handle correctly.

This isn't an argument against off-the-shelf tools. It's an argument for being clear-eyed about what you're actually buying.

Start with the question: does my workflow look like the workflow this tool was designed for?

If yes — use the tool. Don't hire us. It'll be faster and cheaper.

If no — that's when a custom build starts to make sense.

What's your experience been with off-the-shelf AI tools? Where have they fit cleanly, and where have they fallen short?
