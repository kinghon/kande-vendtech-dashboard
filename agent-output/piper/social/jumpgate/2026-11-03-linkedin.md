You've probably started seeing the phrase "agentic commerce" in AI coverage.

The idea: AI agents that don't just analyze data or draft copy, but actually take actions — placing orders, updating records, responding to leads, routing tasks — as part of your commercial operations. The coverage treats it like something on the horizon.

We've been running it since last year.

Our outreach system (Scout + Relay) has processed over 800 prospects, maintains an 84%+ open rate, flags stale leads, generates coaching cards for follow-up calls, and syncs with our internal pipeline in real time. It doesn't ask what to do next. It does it.

Here's what that year taught us:

**The tech is not the hard part.** Connecting an AI agent to a CRM, an email provider, and a search API is genuinely approachable now. The LangChain ecosystem, well-designed prompts, and a modular architecture get you 80% of the way there in weeks.

**The hard part is what happens when the agent is wrong.** Silent failures. Misrouted leads. Coaching that was accurate on Tuesday but stale by Friday because the prospect opened the email again. We've hit every one of those. Built systems to catch them. Had to redesign handoffs twice.

**The even harder part is the second year.** Once the agents are running, the question isn't "can we build this?" It's "how do we know if it's degrading?" Calibration, temporal pattern detection, confidence scoring — these are the problems production systems face that pilot projects never encounter.

If "agentic commerce" is on your radar for 2027, it's worth being clear about what you're actually signing up for. We're happy to talk through what we've built and where the real friction is.
