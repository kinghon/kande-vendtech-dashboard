I set up a vending machine at a medical clinic in Henderson last month. Two machines, actually — one in the staff break room, one in the waiting area.

The nurse manager walked me through the waiting room on my way in. She said: "Family members sit here for 3-4 hours sometimes. There's no café. There's no restaurant nearby. The nearest drive-through is 10 minutes away."

That's not a vending problem. That's a logistics problem that happens to have a vending solution.

The break room machine made sense on its own. 12-hour nursing shifts, limited lunch options, people who physically can't leave the floor during their break. Standard case for any employer account.

But the waiting area machine is different. Those aren't employees making a quick snack run. Those are family members who showed up with nothing and are going to be there a while.

Same property. Two completely different use cases. Two separate restocking cycles, two separate product mixes.

If you manage a medical facility in Las Vegas or Henderson and your vending situation is a single machine in the corner of a hallway — you're probably leaving one of those two audiences without a real option.
