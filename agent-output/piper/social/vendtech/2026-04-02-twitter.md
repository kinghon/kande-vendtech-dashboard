## Recommended Tweet (single)

We turn down locations.

A 200-unit apartment with a broken common room elevator, 40% vacancy, and a PM who won't return emails after three tries? That's not a bad pitch — it's a bad fit.

A machine that sits idle hurts both sides. So we ask:
- Is there foot traffic past the machine?
- Is there management actually engaged in resident experience?
- Does the building's lease-up trajectory make sense?

If the answer is no, we pass.

The vendors who take every location are the ones that eventually pull machines mid-lease because the math doesn't work. We'd rather tell you upfront.

(And yes — when we pass, we usually tell the PM why. Some of them fix it and call us back.)
