## Draft

We're starting to have a different kind of conversation with some of our properties.

Not "do you want a vending machine" — but "have you thought about replacing the machine with a micro market?"

For most property managers, that sounds like a bigger footprint, more complexity, more things to manage. And honestly, that was my concern too when we first started offering them.

But the data on this is pretty clear: consumers spend 53% more at micro markets than at traditional vending machines. That's from Cantaloupe's 2024 research — and it tracks with what we're seeing on-site.

The difference isn't the products. It's the experience. Open shelving, a real cooler section, a self-checkout kiosk. It looks like a corner store, not a vending machine. And people shop it differently.

For a property running a standard single machine doing, say, $400–$500 a month in sales — a micro market in the right common area can push that to $700–$900 without changing a single product in the mix.

The install is still free on our end. The footprint is bigger (usually 8–10 square feet vs. a standard machine's 4), but for communities with a lobby, fitness center, or resident lounge — it fits.

If you're in Henderson or Las Vegas and your residents are already using your current machine consistently, it's worth a conversation.

What questions are you seeing from residents about amenities heading into spring lease-up?
