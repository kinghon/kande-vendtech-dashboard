Here's something most vending companies won't tell you:

The best vending deals aren't one property at a time.

They're portfolio deals.

When a property management company manages 3, 5, or 15 apartment communities, each property has the same basic need: a reliable amenity for residents and staff. Snacks, drinks, convenience items — in the lobby, the clubhouse, or the leasing office.

But each property is also solving it separately. Different vendors (or no vendor). Different machine quality. Different service responsiveness. Some have a machine from 2012 that only takes quarters. Some have nothing.

Portfolio vending changes that:

→ One vendor across all properties
→ Standardized machine quality and payment options
→ Centralized reporting — see what sells where, at which property
→ One point of contact for service, restocking, and maintenance
→ Consistent resident experience across the portfolio

For the regional property manager, this is the difference between managing 8 separate vending relationships and managing one.

We've been working with property management companies in Las Vegas on exactly this model. The pitch isn't "do you want a vending machine?" It's "we can upgrade every property in your Las Vegas portfolio at zero cost, with smart machines that accept contactless payment, and you'll have a single account manager for all of them."

The conversations that go from one-property inquiry to portfolio-wide deal are the ones that create real scale for everyone involved.

If you manage multiple properties in Las Vegas and want to standardize your vending amenity, that's literally what we do.
