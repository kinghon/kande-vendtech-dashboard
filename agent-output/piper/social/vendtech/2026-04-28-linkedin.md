## Post

One thing I've noticed talking to coworking operators and flexible office managers: the vending conversation always starts the same way.

"We tried that once. The machine sat empty for two weeks and nobody came to fix it."

I hear this more than almost any other objection in the apartment or office markets. And honestly — I get it. The economics of coworking vending aren't attractive to the big national players. A 40-member coworking location doesn't get priority on anyone's route. So they stock it once, check in when they feel like it, and leave you with a machine that becomes a point of friction instead of a perk.

What's different about working with a local operator: we're in Las Vegas. We're not routing a service van from a regional depot. If something's wrong with a machine, we know about it and we can get there the same day.

We've been expanding into coworking and flexible office locations over the past few months — there's real demand in the Hughes Center corridor and the Summerlin business parks. If you're running one of these spaces and your current vendor situation is the empty-machine-for-two-weeks story, it might be worth a conversation.
