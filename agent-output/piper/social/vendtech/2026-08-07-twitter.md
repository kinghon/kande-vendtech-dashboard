## 3-Tweet Thread

**Tweet 1:**
A 700-unit garden-style apartment complex sounds impressive for a vending placement.

It often isn't.

Scattered buildings. Multiple lobbies. No single choke point. Residents get packages delivered to their door and rarely pass the common area.

**Tweet 2:**
A 300-unit high-rise with one lobby and one elevator bank?

That's force exposure. Every resident passes that machine. Usage is predictable. The math works.

We actually turn down locations when the traffic pattern doesn't support a machine. Not because we're picky — because an underperforming machine doesn't serve anyone well.

**Tweet 3:**
The metric we use when evaluating a placement: choke point traffic, not unit count.

If there's a natural spot where residents or employees flow through daily — lobby, break room, elevator bank, mailroom — that's where a machine works. Big numbers without that structure just means a machine that sits half-empty.
