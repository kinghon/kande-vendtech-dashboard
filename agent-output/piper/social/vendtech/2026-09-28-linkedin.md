## Draft

We got a call last month from a facilities manager at a local call center. Three shifts. 200 seats. The vending machine the previous vendor installed was a traditional cold drink unit, cash-only, last restocked on a Wednesday.

By Thursday night second shift had nothing. By Friday morning third shift was going to the gas station two blocks away on their break.

He'd been "meaning to fix it" for six months.

Here's the thing about break rooms in 24/7 operations: they're not an amenity. They're infrastructure. When they fail, people notice — not by filing a complaint, but by leaving. Las Vegas has a tight labor market for experienced call center workers. The people who can find another job will find one.

The fix isn't complicated. Smart machines, real-time inventory monitoring, cashless payments, and a restocking schedule that actually accounts for three shifts instead of one. We did the walk-through, mapped machine locations to shift traffic patterns, and had equipment installed in about two weeks.

Zero cost to them. We earn our margin from product sales.

If you're running a 24/7 operation in Las Vegas and your break room is still running on a Monday/Wednesday/Friday schedule, it's worth a conversation.
