Vending Machine Service for Industrial Parks in Las Vegas

Industrial parks are one of the most productive vending location categories in any market, and Las Vegas's industrial park inventory is growing faster than almost any market in the western United States. The combination of large tenant workforces, shift-based operations, and the kind of captive break room environment where leaving for food is not practical creates a consistent, high-volume demand for vending that industrial park tenants across the valley benefit from. Kande VendTech serves industrial park tenants and property managers across Las Vegas at zero cost.

Las Vegas industrial parks range from the premium Class A logistics complexes along the I-15 and I-215 corridors, developments like the Speedway Commerce Center, the North Las Vegas industrial parks around Craig and Lamb, and the Henderson/Sloan logistics zone, to mid-tier multi-tenant industrial parks serving smaller manufacturing, light industrial, and distribution tenants across the valley. The tenant mix varies from single large occupants to multi-tenant facilities where 20-40 individual businesses share a property. Both the large single-occupant industrial and the multi-tenant park present viable vending opportunities, and the approach differs depending on the occupancy structure.

Single-Tenant vs. Multi-Tenant Industrial Park Vending

For single large-tenant industrial facilities, the vending model is straightforward: we assess the workforce size, the break room configuration, and the operational hours, and we place machines sized appropriately for the headcount and shift structure. A 200,000-square-foot distribution facility with 150 employees across two shifts gets a different configuration than a 50,000-square-foot light manufacturing operation with 40 daytime employees. Both can qualify for free service if their workforce generates sufficient daily machine volume.

Multi-tenant industrial parks present a more complex but often more productive vending opportunity. A park with 15 tenants averaging 20 employees each has 300 total workers on-site, which is more than sufficient for productive vending placement, but no individual tenant may meet the solo-placement threshold. For these parks, common area placement, machines in shared lobby or break areas accessible to all tenants, combines the individual tenant populations into a single effective consumer base. Industrial park property managers can implement this approach as a tenant amenity under the park's management authority rather than requiring each tenant to negotiate separately.

At Kande VendTech, we work with both individual industrial tenants and with industrial park property managers on common-area placements. For property managers who want to offer vending as a standard tenant amenity across their park, our portfolio approach allows a single service agreement to cover the full property with machines positioned in common areas and break facilities that serve all tenants simultaneously. This approach is particularly effective in parks that have a communal break area, a shared lobby, or a common truck court restroom and break structure that all tenants use.
