Agentic Commerce: What Small Businesses Should Know in 2026

AI agents are beginning to make purchasing decisions on behalf of consumers. Bain projects they will account for 25% of U.S. ecommerce by 2030. Here is what small businesses need to understand before their customers stop browsing.

  • • Brands that integrate with agentic purchase flows will have a structural advantage over those that require manual browsing (McKinsey Agentic Commerce Report 2025).

The way customers find, evaluate, and purchase products is changing at a pace that most small businesses have not yet registered. For decades, the customer journey moved from awareness to consideration to purchase, with a human making every step of that decision. Increasingly, that is not what is happening. A growing share of research and evaluation is being done by AI agents acting on a human's behalf, and in a meaningful portion of cases, the purchase itself is too.

This is what analysts and industry researchers are calling agentic commerce. It is not a future prediction. It is a pattern that is already showing up in transaction data. Understanding what it means, and what it requires of businesses that want to reach customers through these new channels, is becoming a practical operational question.

At Jumpgate Industries, we work at the intersection of AI agent technology and business operations. What is happening in agentic commerce is relevant not just as a trend to watch but as a set of concrete changes that will affect how small businesses attract and retain customers over the next several years.

What Agentic Commerce Actually Is

Agentic commerce refers to commercial transactions that are initiated, researched, or completed by AI agents operating on behalf of human principals. In the simplest case, a consumer uses an AI assistant to find the best price on a specific product and the assistant provides a recommendation. In more advanced cases, the consumer grants the agent permission to complete the purchase directly within defined parameters. The human sets the budget and criteria; the agent handles the transaction.

This is happening faster than most businesses expect. ChatGPT reached 800 million weekly active users in late 2025, making it one of the most rapidly adopted consumer technologies in history. A substantial portion of those users are using it as a shopping assistant: researching products, comparing vendors, asking for recommendations, and increasingly completing tasks that previously required visiting individual company websites or making phone calls.

McKinsey's 2025 agentic commerce report put it directly: in an agentic world, a business's customer may no longer be a human with a browser. It is just as likely to be an autonomous agent acting on that customer's behalf. For businesses whose sales process depends on capturing attention through traditional web browsing, search engine visibility, and conversion rate optimization, this represents a significant shift in how customers actually arrive and what they look for when they do.

The Consumer Readiness Data

Consumer readiness for agentic purchasing is further along than most businesses realize. Salesforce research found that 44% of U.S. customers would use an AI agent as a personal shopping assistant. Among Gen Z, that number rises to 70%. When broken down by task, 24% of consumers said they would rely on AI agents to handle shopping tasks, and 36% would favor automated purchases over human communication for routine transactions.

Consumer Readiness for AI Shopping Agents

  • • 70% of Gen Z would use AI for shopping (Salesforce)
  • • 36% prefer automated purchases over human communication for routine transactions (Salesforce)
  • • 73% expect brands to use AI to understand their needs (Swap Commerce 2025)
  • • 67% of consumers trust human-like AI interaction (Salesforce)

The trust pattern is also important. Salesforce found that 67% of consumers trust human-like AI interaction, and 68% prefer AI that feels more personal. Thirty-seven percent feel confident about AI generating personalized content tailored to their preferences. Consumer trust in AI shopping agents is not a distant future state. It is already present in a significant portion of the purchasing population, and it is growing faster among younger consumers who will represent the majority of purchasing power within a decade.

What This Means for Small Business Visibility

If a meaningful share of purchases in your market segment move through agentic channels, the traditional approaches to customer acquisition become less effective. Search engine optimization for human browsers becomes less relevant when the agent is not using a browser in the traditional sense. Conversion rate optimization of a landing page matters less when the agent never visits the landing page. The visual design of your website matters less when the agent is consuming structured data, not HTML.

What matters in agentic commerce is structured, accessible, accurate information about your products and services in formats that AI agents can consume and evaluate. This includes API accessibility, structured product data, clear pricing, and well-documented terms. It also includes presence in the data sources that AI agents are trained on and retrieve from, which overlaps with but is not identical to traditional SEO.

The businesses that will have a structural advantage in agentic commerce are those whose products and services are easy for AI agents to find, evaluate, and transact with. Businesses that require a phone call to get basic pricing information, whose websites have no structured data markup, and whose product descriptions are optimized for visual appeal rather than information density will be at a disadvantage when agents are making the research and purchase decisions.

What Small Businesses Should Do Now

The full shift to agentic commerce is still in its early stages, but the preparation work is available and concrete now.

First, make sure your core product and pricing information is structured and accessible. Implement schema.org markup on product pages. Ensure pricing is clearly stated on your website rather than "call for a quote" wherever possible. Make sure your business information, service descriptions, and terms are clear and complete. This benefits both traditional SEO and agentic discoverability.

Second, think about your responsiveness to AI-generated inquiries. As agentic commerce grows, a portion of your inbound inquiries will be generated by AI agents on behalf of human prospects. These inquiries will be structured, specific, and expect fast responses. Businesses that respond quickly with accurate information will convert more of this traffic than businesses that route these inquiries through slow manual processes.

Third, understand that customer relationships in an agentic world still matter, but the locus of influence shifts. If a customer's AI agent makes routine purchases on their behalf, the human customer relationship determines which brands the agent is authorized to purchase from. Brand preference, customer satisfaction, and relationship quality become more important rather than less, because they determine whether a customer's agent is even allowed to buy from you.
