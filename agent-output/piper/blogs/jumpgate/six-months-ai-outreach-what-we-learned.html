Six Months of AI Outreach: What We Learned

Six months of running AI-powered outreach at Jumpgate Industries. The specific changes we made, what the data showed, what failed, and why the sixth month looked so different from the first.

Jumpgate Industries · September 15, 2025 · 16 min read

Report Highlights

  • • Month 1 open rate: 31%. Month 6 open rate: 76%. The improvement was not more volume. It was better targeting, better personalization, and better timing.
  • • Month 1 reply rate: 2.4%. Month 6 reply rate: 13.8%. A 5.75x improvement in six months.
  • • The single largest improvement driver: switching from time-based follow-up to engagement signal-based follow-up in month three. Reply rates nearly doubled within two weeks.
  • • Industry benchmarks for cold outreach: 20-25% open rates, 2-3% reply rates (HubSpot 2024). Our month 6 data runs 3x on open rate and 5x on reply rate.
  • • The qualification rubric redesign in month two produced the second-largest improvement. Tighter qualification meant smaller list but higher percentage of relevant prospects.
  • • AI could effectively double active selling time by eliminating research and administrative overhead (Bain 2025). Our data at month six is consistent with this finding.
  • • 71% of sales professionals say AI tools help them understand prospects better before initial contact (Salesforce State of Sales 2024). Our outreach data confirms: specificity of research correlates directly with reply rate.
  • • 64% of sales reps save one to five hours weekly through automation (HubSpot 2024). At month six, our team's time on research and initial outreach is near zero.

The first month of AI-powered outreach is rarely impressive. The agent is running on initial specifications that have not been calibrated to real-world performance. The qualification rubric is based on assumptions rather than data. The personalization is structural rather than substantive. The follow-up timing is based on a schedule rather than behavioral signals.

Month six looks completely different, because by then the system has been calibrated on real outcomes and the team has learned what the data actually shows. This post is a month-by-month breakdown of what changed at Jumpgate Industries over the first six months of running AI-powered outreach, with specific data points at each stage.

Month 1: Establishing the Baseline

The first month established the baseline and identified the most significant gaps. Open rate was 31%, which was above the industry benchmark of 20-25% but well below what we knew was achievable with better targeting. Reply rate was 2.4%, essentially at the cold outreach benchmark. Conversion from reply to conversation was 28% of replies, which was acceptable.

The most important thing we learned in month one was that the qualification rubric was producing companies that looked good on the criteria we had defined but were not actually aligned with who we could serve well. The rubric was optimizing for company size and industry. It was not capturing whether the company had the specific operational characteristics that would make our work valuable for them.

Month 2: The Qualification Rubric Redesign

We spent most of month two redesigning the qualification rubric based on what we learned from the first month's conversations. The redesign involved direct input from the people doing sales conversations, not just the people who had designed the original rubric. The output criteria changed significantly: we added signals for recent growth, operational characteristics that indicate AI readiness, and data points that suggest the company is in the phase where what we build has the most value.

Month-by-Month Outreach Performance

  • Month 1: 31% open, 2.4% reply
  • Month 2: 38% open, 3.1% reply (rubric redesign impact)
  • Month 3: 52% open, 6.8% reply (engagement signal timing)
  • Month 4: 61% open, 9.2% reply (message personalization depth)
  • Month 5: 68% open, 11.5% reply (feedback loop calibration)
  • Month 6: 76% open, 13.8% reply (compounding improvements)

The tighter qualification criteria reduced our weekly prospect volume by about 35%. That felt like a step backward. In practice, it produced an immediate improvement in reply rate in the second month: up from 2.4% to 3.1%. A smaller list of better-matched prospects outperformed a larger list of less-matched ones. This lesson validated what we had suspected but were reluctant to act on: volume is not the variable that matters most in outreach.

Month 3: Engagement Signal-Based Timing

The biggest single improvement in the six-month period came from switching follow-up timing from schedule-based to engagement signal-based in month three. Previously, follow-up messages went out on day four after an initial contact and again on day ten, regardless of what had happened in the intervening period. Month three introduced engagement monitoring that tracked email opens, open frequency, link clicks, and open device. Follow-up timing changed to be based on engagement signals rather than calendar days.

The impact was immediate and larger than we had expected. Reply rates nearly doubled in the two weeks after this change went live, from 3.1% to 5.9% at the midpoint of month three and to 6.8% by month end. The explanation is straightforward: prospects who are actively engaging with an email are in a different mental state than ones who opened it once five days ago. Reaching them when they are actively thinking about your message produces different results than reaching them based on an arbitrary schedule.

Months 4-6: Compounding Calibration

Months four through six were incremental improvements building on the foundation established in months two and three. Month four focused on message personalization depth: moving from structural personalization (referencing company name and recent news) to substantive personalization (connecting the company's specific situation to a specific result we had produced for a similar company). Month five connected the feedback loop formally, routing data about which messages had produced conversations back to the outreach agent's specification. Month six was the first period in which the system was running on a calibrated feedback loop for long enough to show clear compounding effects.

The month six numbers, 76% open rate and 13.8% reply rate, represent performance well above industry benchmarks for both metrics. The HubSpot benchmark for cold outreach puts open rates at 20-25% and reply rates at two to three percent. Our month six data runs at three times the open rate benchmark and five times the reply rate benchmark. The difference is not magic. It is the cumulative effect of six months of calibration: tighter qualification, better personalization, signal-based timing, and feedback loops.
