## 💡 Insights Generated

### Insight 1: Construction Sites Are a New Lead Category — "Project Duration" Replaces "Annual Contract"
Hard Rock Las Vegas (600 → 1,800 workers, 2–3 year project) introduces a contract model that doesn't exist elsewhere in the pipeline. Residential deals are month-to-month or 1-year. Construction sites are project-duration — 2–3 years locked at project start, with scaling revenue as workforce peaks. The switching cost mid-project is structural, not relational. This makes early installation the most powerful strategy: get in during mobilization phase (now, at 600 workers) before the site is operationally crowded. Revenue math at 1,800-worker peak rivals a Portfolio Brand Program. Decision-maker: site superintendent at Turner Construction's Las Vegas project office — not Hard Rock International corporate.

### Insight 2: The Express Priority Rule Applies to Construction Project Start, Not Opening Day
The "new facility under 90 days" express priority was designed for property openings. For construction sites, the equivalent trigger is **project mobilization** — the first 60–90 days of a major build. Reason: vendor relationships formed during mobilization become embedded in site operations. By the time the workforce peaks, a vendor change requires operational disruption. Rule update: add `property_type: construction_site` to CRM schema. Any construction site in mobilization phase gets the same express priority treatment as a newly-opened facility.

### Insight 3: Data Center Workers = Dialysis Center Economics With a Tech Twist
Novva Data Center (150–300 employees, 24/7, zero-egress policy during shift) is the highest-income captive audience Scout has found outside of tech campuses. The captive audience economics mirror dialysis (can't leave, long hours) but the demographic skews toward tech/engineering workers who expect premium snack options. Pitch track: employer break room, but with premium product framing. Decision-maker: facilities manager or director of IT operations, not a property manager. Email-first works (unlike construction pop-in), but requires finding direct contact before sending — generic info@ kills this lead. Tag: `channel: call_first` after contact research, `pitch_track: employer_break_room`.

### Insight 4: Opportunity Village NW Campus = Nurture-to-Call_First Pipeline Prototype for Nonprofits
OV's three existing campuses mean they have active vendor relationships that will default-extend to the new campus unless Kande establishes a relationship during construction. The pitch window is 60 days before the fall 2026 opening — not at opening. Dual-placement potential: staff break room + client-accessible machine = healthcare_dual economics for a nonprofit. Tag now as `channel: nurture, follow_up_date: 2026-08-01`. Convert to call_first in August. This is also the first nonprofit to map to `pitch_track: developer_preleasing` — OV operates like a developer during construction, then like a healthcare facility after opening.

### Insight 5: Employer-Category Leads Fill the Negotiating Stage Gap Faster Than Residential Portfolio Deals
Only 1 prospect in Negotiating (Carnegie Heights). Residential portfolio deals (Aspire/Ovation, EVO Apartments) require multi-stakeholder approval chains — corporate, property management, possibly HOA. Employer-category deals (Hard Rock/Turner Construction, Novva Data Center) require a single facilities decision. Expected proposal-to-negotiating timeline: employer = 1–2 weeks. Residential portfolio = 3–6 weeks. To fill the Negotiating stage to 3–5 deals by April, prioritize Hard Rock and Novva as fast-cycle employer deals alongside the residential portfolio work.
