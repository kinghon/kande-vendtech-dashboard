## 💡 Insights Generated

**Insight 1: Smith's Food & Drug = Employer Reference Chain Anchor**
The residential portfolio track compounds through management company relationships (Ovation → 36 communities). The employer track compounds through reference selling across similar employers in the same geography. Smith's Henderson (250-300 employees, opened Jan 25) is the right anchor for this chain: Smith's close → EōS Fitness Henderson → Paysign Henderson → Rapid Response Monitoring. Three employers, one geographic cluster, one credibility chain. The call framing today isn't just "close Smith's" — it's "open the employer reference chain." Jordan should treat this as a showcase account request, not a cold pitch.

**Insight 2: Employer Pitch Track Is Operationally Distinct — Never Use Amenity Framing**
The entire residential playbook (amenity argument, resident satisfaction, community feel) fails in an employer context. The break room / shift worker pitch is: "Your employees work around the clock and break rooms are their only option during shift. We eliminate the dead time lost to off-site food runs, keep energy up on late shifts, and cost you nothing to install." Decision-maker is a Store Director or Facilities Manager — not a property manager. Initial ask is a site visit, not a proposal.

**Insight 3: Healthcare Dual-Placement = Two Machines Per Sales Call**
Any healthcare clinic with a separate staff zone and a patient waiting area supports two placements: staff break room (same pitch as employer) + lobby placement (captive audience, 45-90 min waits). The staff interruption angle is the opener: "Patients asking where the snack machine is = dead clinical time. We put the machine where they can find it, so your staff doesn't have to redirect them." CCHC Nevada is the first live test of this template. If it closes as a dual placement, it becomes the healthcare standard pitch.

**Insight 4: 6-Day Vendor Window — Express Protocol for New Healthcare Facilities**
CCHC Nevada opens March 2 (6 days from now). Healthcare clinics in their first 90 days are in active vendor selection. After 90 days, an incumbent is locked in. The express protocol: source local contact today, call before opening, frame as "easier to have facilities sorted before day one." This is the same timing-urgency logic validated by Gemma Las Vegas (opening_soon → active window). New healthcare facilities belong on the same status-change monitoring that Scout runs via `GET /api/crm/status-diff`.

**Insight 5: The CRM "New" Bucket Is Where Call-First Leads Go to Die**
Scout's HOT and WARM leads (Smith's, CCHC) are being added to the same CRM "new" bucket as 400 cold stale leads from February 2-12. If Relay doesn't manually catch them in the morning review, they get lost in the standard Instantly sequence. Short-term fix: Relay manually reviews all Scout HOT/WARM leads each morning and adds them to the call sheet. Long-term fix: Scout tags leads as `channel: call_first` vs `channel: campaign` at research time — routes them to the call sheet directly, never to Instantly.

**Insight 6: Stage=Null Is a Business Intelligence Blackout, Not Just an Admin Task**
With 529 prospects at stage=null, the account tier page shows $0 MRR, the funnel is invisible, and there's no way to track Smith's and CCHC from "new" to "proposal" to "close." The immediate 15-minute fix: bulk-tag the 10-15 known portfolio plays (Ovation, Greystar, RPM, Siegel, Calida, C&W) as Portfolio Brand Program.
