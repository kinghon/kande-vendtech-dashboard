# Email 0 Draft Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

_Updated 2026-04-09 based on Kurtis's actual sent emails_

## Contact Names
- ALWAYS .title() case names. Never ALL CAPS. "Lorena" not "LORENA".
- Names are stored ALL CAPS in the CRM. The script MUST convert before using.

## First Sentence (MANDATORY FORMAT)
Must include WHO Jordan spoke with, WHEN, and end with the standard phrase.

**If Jordan spoke with the email recipient directly:**
"Jordan from my team let me know he spoke with you [when] and you were interested in getting more info about our free smart vending machines!"

**If Jordan spoke with someone ELSE at the location (staff, front desk, etc.):**
"Jordan from my team let me know he spoke with [Name] ([role]) at [Location] [when] and [he/she/they] [was/were] interested in getting more info about our free smart vending machines!"

Examples from real emails:
- "he spoke with Suzette (front office) today and she was interested..."
- "he spoke with Michael (front desk) at Otonomus Hotel today and they were interested..."
- "he spoke with Suzanne (front desk) last Friday and she was interested..."
- "he spoke with Chris at the leasing office this past Friday and he was interested..."

**If Jordan stopped by but didn't speak with anyone specific:**
"Jordan from my team let me know he stopped by at [Location] [when] and your team was interested in getting more info about our free smart vending machines!"

## Second Sentence (ONLY when relevant notes exist)
Pull specific details from the pop-in notes. Examples from real emails:
- Fresh food interest: "She mentioned having our fresh food products would be an upgrade to your current solution."
- Excited about amenity: "He was really excited about having food/snacks easily available for residents and staff."
- Asked about vending status: "You mentioned inquiring about the current vending status and amenities"
- Warehouse/industrial: "We offer fresh foods like sandwiches, wraps, and salads that would be a great added amenity for your warehouse staff."
- Wants proposal: "As discussed, I've included a proposal for your review."
- Wants samples: "We'd also love to set up a sample tasting when you're ready."
- Needs corporate approval: "I understand this may need approval from your corporate team, so I've put together all the details to make that process easy."
- Has existing vendor: "I understand you may have an existing vendor arrangement. We'd love the opportunity to show you what modern smart vending looks like when you're ready to explore options."
- Contact was busy: "I know things can get busy, so I wanted to follow up with some info you can review at your convenience."

**If no notable details in the notes: skip the second sentence entirely. Don't force it.**

## Closer of Opening Paragraph
Always: "I wanted to reach out and send over a bit more info for you and your team to review. I've attached a proposal doc for you."

## Before Creating Drafts (DUPLICATE CHECK)
- Check Gmail for ANY email to this contact's address in the last 2 days (not just "in:sent" — also check inbox for Jordan's emails that were CC'd/forwarded).
- If found, DO NOT create a draft. Skip this contact.
- Check if Email 0 was already sent for this prospect. If yes, skip.

## Template Structure
1. "Hey [Name]," (properly capitalized)
2. First sentence (who Jordan spoke with + when + "interested in getting more info about our free smart vending machines!")
3. Second sentence ONLY if relevant details exist in notes
4. "I wanted to reach out and send over a bit more info..."
5. Standard benefits bullets (unchanged)
6. Proposal attachment
7. Call-to-action: "After you have a chance to review our proposal, I'd love to chat about how we can meet your needs. Are you free for a quick call in the next couple of days?"

## Quality Checks
- Zero ALL CAPS names
- Zero em dashes
- Correct "when" reference (today/yesterday/on Monday/last Friday)
- Staff member named with role in parentheses when Jordan spoke with someone other than the recipient
- Proposal PDF attached
- Sent from kurtis@kandevendtech.com
- CC: jordan@kandevendtech.com
