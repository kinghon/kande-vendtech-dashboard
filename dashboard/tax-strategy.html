The Big One: Deduct Machines Immediately

Under current tax law, you can deduct the full cost of vending machines in Year 1 ‚Äî either through Section 179 or 100% Bonus Depreciation. This means if you buy 10 machines at $4,000 each, you get a $40,000 deduction in the year of purchase.

  • Section 179 limit (2025): $1,220,000 ‚Äî more than enough for any vending operation
  • Bonus Depreciation (2025): 100% ‚Äî deduct the full cost immediately
  • MACRS classification: Vending machines = 5-year property (if you don't use 179/bonus)
  • Applies to: New AND used equipment

üèõÔ∏è S-Corp Election Strategy

When to Elect S-Corp Status

As a 2-member LLC, you're currently taxed as a partnership. Once your net profit exceeds ~$40,000-$50,000/year, consider electing S-Corp status to save on self-employment tax.

  • Current: All profit subject to 15.3% self-employment tax
  • With S-Corp: Pay yourself a "reasonable salary" ‚Üí only salary is subject to SE tax, remaining profit is not
  • Example: $100K profit ‚Üí $50K salary + $50K distribution = save ~$7,650/year in SE tax
  • Downside: Must run payroll, file S-Corp return (Form 1120S), more CPA costs
  • When: File Form 2553 by March 15 of the tax year you want it effective
üíé QBI Deduction (20% Pass-Through)

Free 20% Deduction on Business Income

The Qualified Business Income deduction lets you deduct 20% of your net business income from your taxable income. For vending, this is essentially free money.

  • Example: $80K net profit √ó 20% = $16,000 deduction = ~$3,840 tax savings (at 24%)
  • Phase-out: Starts at $182,100 (single) / $364,200 (MFJ) ‚Äî not an issue early on
  • Vending qualifies: It's NOT a "specified service trade" so no limitations apply
